# ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — AI Management System (AIMS) Compliance Reference

## Overview
ISO/IEC 42001 is the first international standard for Artificial Intelligence Management Systems.
It follows the same high-level structure (HLS/Annex SL) as ISO 9001 and ISO 27001, making it
integrable into existing management system frameworks. It is certification-eligible.

## Key Concepts

- **AIMS**: AI Management System — the overall system of policies, processes, and controls
- **AI System Impact Assessment**: structured evaluation of the system's potential effects
- **AI Policy**: top-level organizational commitment to responsible AI
- **Interested parties**: anyone affected by or with a stake in the AI system
- **Objectives**: measurable goals the AIMS is designed to achieve

---

## Reviewer Checklist Questions by Stage

### Context & Planning (Clause 4–6)
- Has the organization defined its internal and external context for AI use?
- Are interested parties (users, regulators, affected communities) identified and their needs understood?
- Has an AI Policy been established and communicated?
- Are AI-specific risks and opportunities documented in the risk register?
- Are AI objectives measurable and aligned with organizational strategy?

### Leadership (Clause 5)
- Has top management demonstrated commitment to the AIMS?
- Is there a named management representative or AI governance role?
- Has the AI Policy been communicated to all relevant staff?
- Are accountability structures for AI decisions documented?

### Support (Clause 7)
- Are there sufficient resources (people, tools, budget) dedicated to the AIMS?
- Do staff involved in AI have documented competencies and training records?
- Is there a communication plan for AI-related information (internal and external)?
- Is documented information (policies, procedures, records) controlled and maintained?

### Operation (Clause 8)
- Has an **AI System Impact Assessment** been completed for this system?
- Are operational plans and controls in place for the AI system?
- Has supply chain / third-party AI risk been assessed and managed?
- Is there a process for managing changes to the AI system?

### Performance Evaluation (Clause 9)
- Are monitoring and measurement processes defined for the AIMS?
- Is there a schedule for internal audits of the AIMS?
- Has management review of the AIMS been conducted and documented?
- Are KPIs for AI performance tracked and reported?

### Improvement (Clause 10)
- Are nonconformities identified, documented, and corrected?
- Is root cause analysis conducted for AI-related incidents or failures?
- Is there a continual improvement plan for the AIMS?

---

## Mandatory Sign-Off Questions (Certification-Track)

These items are typically required for ISO 42001 Stage 1 or Stage 2 audit readiness:

1. An AI Policy exists, is approved by top management, and is accessible to staff.
2. An AI System Impact Assessment has been completed and reviewed.
3. AI-related risks and opportunities are documented in the risk register.
4. Internal audit has been conducted (or is scheduled) within the past 12 months.
5. A management review meeting has been held and minuted.
6. Nonconformities from previous audits are tracked and resolved.

## Integration with Other Standards

ISO 42001 uses the same HLS as ISO 27001 and ISO 9001. If your org already has a certified
ISMS or QMS, many of the infrastructure clauses (4–7, 9–10) can be integrated rather than
rebuilt from scratch.

| ISO 42001 Clause | ISO 27001 Equivalent | ISO 9001 Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| 4 — Context | 4 | 4 |
| 5 — Leadership | 5 | 5 |
| 6 — Planning | 6 | 6 |
| 7 — Support | 7 | 7 |
| 8 — Operation | 8 | 8 |
| 9 — Performance eval | 9 | 9 |
| 10 — Improvement | 10 | 10 |

Annex A and B controls are AI-specific and have no direct equivalents in other standards.
