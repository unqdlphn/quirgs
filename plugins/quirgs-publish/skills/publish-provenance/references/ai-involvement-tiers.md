# AI Involvement Tiers

Five-tier classification of AI's role in music creation, with copyright implications and platform requirements.

---

## Tier Definitions

### Tier 0 — No AI
**Definition:** The work was created entirely by humans using conventional music production tools (DAWs, instruments, microphones, standard software effects and plugins). No AI-powered generation tools were used at any stage of creation.

**Includes:**
- Works produced using traditional DAWs (Logic Pro, Pro Tools, Ableton Live, etc.)
- Works using standard effects plugins (reverb, compression, EQ) — these are not AI
- Works using quantization, auto-tune, or pitch correction — these are algorithmic tools, not AI generation
- Works using loop libraries (pre-licensed samples) — the sample is AI-free by this classification even if AI was used to create the library

**Copyright status:** Full copyright protection under current US, EU, and international law. No special disclosures required for copyright registration.

**Platform requirements:** No AI disclosure required by any major DSP or social platform as of mid-2026.

---

### Tier 1 — AI-Assisted
**Definition:** AI tools were used to enhance, analyze, or process the work, but no AI-generated content appears in the final release. Human creative authorship is the clear and dominant origin of all musical content.

**Includes:**
- AI-powered mastering (Landr, eMastered, iZotope Ozone's AI assist)
- AI stem separation (Moises, Lalal.ai, RipX) used for remixing from a human original
- AI mix analysis tools (used for reference comparison, not to generate content)
- AI-powered noise reduction or restoration
- AI-powered MIDI correction or groove quantization
- AI composition analysis for key/mode detection
- Using AI to suggest a chord progression that was then entirely re-written by a human

**Copyright status:** Full copyright protection expected. AI tools used for production processing do not affect copyright in the musical work. Same status as Tier 0 for rights purposes.

**Platform requirements:** No disclosure required for Tier 1 tools at most platforms. Some platforms (YouTube, TikTok) may require disclosure if AI tools are considered "synthetic" under their specific policy definitions — check current platform policy.

---

### Tier 2 — AI-Collaborative
**Definition:** AI generated musical content (melody fragments, lyrics, chord progressions, audio stems) that was incorporated into the final work after significant creative modification by a human author. The human's creative selection, modification, and integration of AI-generated content constitutes a substantial creative contribution.

**Includes:**
- AI generated a melodic idea that a human then developed, extended, and arranged into the final composition
- AI wrote a verse of lyrics that a human substantially rewrote (more than minor word changes)
- AI generated stems or loops that a human modified in pitch, rhythm, or arrangement before using
- AI suggested a chord progression that a human used as a starting point, then significantly transformed
- Human used generative AI in a back-and-forth collaborative process, making deliberate creative selections

**Copyright status:** Copyright likely available for the human-authored elements and the creative selection/arrangement as a whole. Documentation of the human's creative decisions is important evidence. US Copyright Office interim guidance (2023–2024) supports copyright in works where human authorship is discernible and substantial.

**Platform requirements:** Disclosure of AI involvement recommended; required by some platforms (TikTok "AI-generated content" label, YouTube synthetic media policy). Check each platform's current policy.

---

### Tier 3 — AI-Directed
**Definition:** AI generated substantial portions of the final work. Human contribution is primarily curatorial — selecting, arranging, and combining AI-generated outputs based on creative vision. Human authorship exists in the curation and arrangement decisions but not in the generated content itself.

**Includes:**
- Human selected from dozens of AI-generated melodies and assembled them into a composition
- Human provided detailed text prompts and accepted AI audio output with minor edits (trimming, ordering)
- Human arranged AI-generated stems into a final mix without significantly altering the generated content
- Human directed the AI's generation process through iterative prompting but did not originate musical material

**Copyright status:** Uncertain under current law. The Copyright Office's AI authorship guidance (as of 2024) does not recognize copyright in AI-generated content itself; copyright may attach to the human's creative arrangement of AI-generated elements if the selection and arrangement constitutes original expression. Legal review recommended before registering or licensing commercially.

**Platform requirements:** Every major platform now has an AI-disclosure mechanism, but mandates vary (as of mid-2026). TikTok requires its AI-generated content label for realistic AI content. YouTube applies automatic AI-detection and labeling (rolled out May–June 2026) on top of its synthetic-media disclosure rules. Spotify accepts AI-involvement metadata through AI Credits (DDEX-based, voluntary beta since April 2026, submitted via distributors). Apple Music accepts Transparency Tags (announced March 2026) — optional and self-declared by labels/distributors. Failure to disclose where labeling is mandatory may result in removal.

---

### Tier 4 — AI-Generated
**Definition:** The work was generated entirely by AI from a text or audio prompt. Human involvement is limited to providing the prompt and accepting the output. No meaningful creative modification by a human is present in the final work.

**Includes:**
- Using Suno, Udio, or similar AI music generation platforms to generate a complete track from a text prompt and releasing the output as-is
- Using a generative AI model to produce stems and combining them without human musical modification
- Generating lyrics with an LLM and generating music separately with an AI tool and releasing both without modification

**Copyright status:** No copyright protection under current US law per Copyright Office guidance. The EU AI Act and emerging regulations do not create copyright where none exists. The work may still be commercially released and licensed, but the publisher/creator cannot register or assert copyright in the AI-generated portions.

**Platform requirements:** Disclosure required by TikTok (label) and applied automatically by YouTube (AI-detection labeling); supported as voluntary structured metadata at Spotify (AI Credits) and Apple Music (Transparency Tags). Some platforms restrict or prohibit AI-generated music in certain categories (e.g., children's content, classical music sections). Review platform-specific policies before distribution. Failure to disclose AI-generated content may result in content removal or account suspension.

## Copyright Implications Summary

| Tier | Copyright in composition | Copyright in sound recording | Copyright in arrangement |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| 1 | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| 2 | Likely (with documentation) | Likely (with documentation) | Yes (if human) |
| 3 | Uncertain | Uncertain | Possibly (selection/arrangement) |
| 4 | No | No | Possibly (if arrangement is independently creative) |

Note: Copyright law in this area is rapidly evolving. The above reflects the state of law as of mid-2025. Consult an IP attorney for current advice.
