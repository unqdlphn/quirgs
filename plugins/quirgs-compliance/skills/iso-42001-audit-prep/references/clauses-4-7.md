# ISO 42001 — Clauses 4–7 Reference (Context, Leadership, Planning, Support)

These four clauses form the **management system foundation**. Stage 1 auditors focus heavily
here — if documentation doesn't exist for these clauses, certification cannot proceed.

---

## Clause 4 — Context of the Organization

### 4.1 Understanding the Organization and Its Context
**Requirement:** Identify internal and external factors relevant to the AIMS purpose and
affected by AI systems.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| Internal factors documented (culture, capabilities, resources, AI strategy) | Context analysis document or strategic plan |
| External factors documented (regulatory, market, stakeholder expectations) | PESTLE or equivalent analysis |
| Factors reviewed periodically | Review meeting minutes or document version history |

**Auditor focus:** "Show me your context analysis. When was it last updated? Does it mention the EU AI Act or NIST?"

---

### 4.2 Understanding the Needs and Expectations of Interested Parties
**Requirement:** Identify stakeholders and their AI-related requirements.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| Stakeholder register exists | Stakeholder register or list |
| Stakeholder requirements relevant to AI are documented | Requirements log or stakeholder analysis |
| Which requirements become AIMS obligations is defined | Policy or procedure referencing obligations |

**Auditor focus:** "Who are your interested parties? What do regulators require of you? How do you track that?"

---

### 4.3 Determining the Scope of the AIMS
**Requirement:** Define what is and is not included in the AIMS.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| Scope statement exists (written, approved) | Scope document |
| Scope includes which AI systems/products are covered | Named AI systems list |
| Exclusions are justified | Scope document with rationale for exclusions |
| Scope is available as documented information | Document control record |

**Auditor focus:** "What is in scope? What's excluded and why? Is this scope statement formally approved?"

---

### 4.4 AI Management System
**Requirement:** Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve the AIMS.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| AIMS processes are identified and their interactions understood | Process map or AIMS framework document |
| AIMS is implemented (not just documented) | Evidence of process operation |

---

## Clause 5 — Leadership

### 5.1 Leadership and Commitment
**Requirement:** Top management demonstrates commitment to the AIMS.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| AI policy established and approved by top management | Signed AI policy with executive signature |
| Resources allocated to AIMS | Budget records, headcount allocation |
| AIMS integrated into business processes | Evidence in project plans, org charts |
| Leadership promotes continual improvement | Management review minutes |

**Auditor focus:** Will often interview a senior leader directly. "What is your personal commitment to responsible AI?"

---

### 5.2 AI Policy
**Requirement:** A documented AI policy exists that is appropriate to the organization.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| Policy commits to satisfying applicable requirements | Policy document |
| Policy commits to continual improvement of AIMS | Policy document |
| Policy is communicated to all relevant parties | Communication records, training logs |
| Policy is available to interested parties | Published location (intranet, public site) |
| Policy covers responsible AI principles | Policy document (fairness, transparency, accountability) |

**Auditor focus:** "Read me your AI policy. Who approved it? How do staff know about it? When was it last reviewed?"

---

### 5.3 Organizational Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities
**Requirement:** AI-related roles and responsibilities are defined and communicated.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| AIMS-relevant roles are documented | Role descriptions, RACI matrix |
| Responsibilities for reporting AIMS performance to management are assigned | Named role in documentation |
| AI risk and oversight responsibilities are clear | Org chart or responsibility matrix |

---

## Clause 6 — Planning

### 6.1 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities
**Requirement:** Risks and opportunities related to the AIMS context are identified and addressed.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| Risks and opportunities are determined (from Clause 4 context) | Risk register |
| Actions are planned to address them | Risk treatment plan |
| Actions are integrated into AIMS processes | Documented process links |
| Effectiveness of actions is evaluated | Review records |

---

### 6.2 AI Objectives and Planning to Achieve Them
**Requirement:** Measurable AI objectives are established and plans made to achieve them.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| AI objectives are defined at relevant functions/levels | Objectives register or policy |
| Objectives are measurable | KPIs or metrics associated with each objective |
| Objectives are monitored and communicated | Performance dashboards, reporting records |
| Plans specify: what, resources, responsible person, timeline, evaluation method | Objective plans document |

**Auditor focus:** "What are your AI objectives? How do you know if you're achieving them? Show me the metrics."

---

### 6.3 AI System Impact Assessment Planning
**Requirement:** Plan how AI system impact assessments will be conducted.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| A process for AI system impact assessment exists | Procedure document |
| Impact assessment triggers are defined (new systems, significant changes) | Procedure document |
| Criteria for assessment are established | Assessment template or methodology |

---

## Clause 7 — Support

### 7.1 Resources
**Requirement:** Resources for AIMS are determined and provided.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| Human resources for AIMS are identified and available | Org chart, staffing plan |
| Infrastructure and technology are available | Asset inventory or IT records |
| Environment for AIMS operation is maintained | Facilities / tooling records |

---

### 7.2 Competence
**Requirement:** Personnel performing AI-related work are competent.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| Required competencies are defined | Competency framework or job descriptions |
| Competencies of relevant personnel are assessed | Assessment records |
| Training or other actions taken where gaps exist | Training records, development plans |
| Competence actions are evaluated for effectiveness | Post-training assessment or manager sign-off |

**Auditor focus:** "How do you know your AI team is competent? Show me the training records."

---

### 7.3 Awareness
**Requirement:** Relevant personnel are aware of the AI policy, their contribution, and implications of non-conformance.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| Awareness program exists | Training plan, awareness campaign records |
| Staff can articulate the AI policy | Spot-check or survey records |
| Staff understand consequences of AIMS non-conformance | Training content |

---

### 7.4 Communication
**Requirement:** Internal and external communication relevant to the AIMS is planned.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| Communication plan exists (what, when, to whom, how) | Communications plan |
| Internal communications are executed | Meeting records, newsletters, intranet posts |
| External communications (transparency notices, disclosures) are managed | Published disclosures |

---

### 7.5 Documented Information
**Requirement:** Required documented information is controlled and maintained.

| Requirement | Evidence needed |
|---|---|
| All required AIMS documents are created | Document register |
| Documents are version-controlled | Document control system records |
| Documents are available to those who need them | Access control records |
| Retention and disposal rules are defined | Records retention policy |

**Auditor focus:** This is high scrutiny. Auditors will pull random documents and check version numbers, approval signatures, and review dates.
