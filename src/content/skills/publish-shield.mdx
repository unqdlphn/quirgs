## What it does

Publish Shield is a two-module pre-release gate. Module A checks the release against current content policies across major DSPs and distributors — Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, TikTok, Amazon Music, and DistroKid — surfacing any disclosure requirements, labeling obligations, or outright restrictions that apply based on the track's AI involvement tier. It issues a per-platform pass/hold/block status and explains the specific policy clause that triggered each flag.

Module B generates a documented AI governance policy for the publishing operation: a written internal policy covering how AI tools are evaluated, approved, and disclosed in creative workflows. The policy is formatted for internal review and can be published publicly as a label transparency statement.

Publish Shield loads the AI tool tier map to infer involvement tier from the disclosed production tools when a tier hasn't been explicitly provided by publish-provenance.

## Frameworks covered

- DSP content policies for AI-generated music (Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, TikTok, Amazon)
- Distributor submission policies (DistroKid, TuneCore, CD Baby)
- EU AI Act Article 13 — transparency for AI-generated outputs
- TikTok AI-generated content labeling standards
- YouTube's synthetic media disclosure requirements

## When to use it

Run Publish Shield as the final gate before any release goes to distribution. A track that passes publish-provenance with a Tier 2 or Tier 3 determination needs platform-specific clearance before delivery — platform policies are updated frequently and vary significantly. Publish Shield also feeds into publish-license: tracks with active platform blocks should not be pitched for sync, regardless of catalog fit score.

Use Module B when building or reviewing the label's AI policy, when a platform or rights society requests documentation of AI use policies, or when publishing governance documentation for audit purposes.
