## What it does

The AI Transparency Writer produces ready-to-publish transparency documentation in two modes: an EU AI Act Article 13 transparency notice for deployers of high-risk AI systems, and a NIST AI RMF-aligned model card for teams that need structured documentation of an AI system's purpose, capabilities, limitations, and risk profile. Both outputs are actual draft documents — not checklists or templates — that can be edited and published directly after legal review.

## Frameworks covered

* EU AI Act Article 13 — transparency and provision of information to deployers
* EU AI Act Article 50 — transparency obligations for certain AI systems (deepfake, emotion recognition)
* NIST AI RMF 1.0 — Govern function, GV-1 and GV-6 subcategories
* NIST AI RMF Playbook — model documentation guidance

## When to use it

A transparency notice under Article 13 is legally required before a high-risk AI system is placed on the EU market or put into service. Deployers must receive clear, intelligible information about the system's intended purpose, its capabilities and limitations, human oversight requirements, and the identity of the provider. Reach for this skill after completing EU AI Act risk classification — once you know you're in Annex III, this notice is a required deliverable. The model card mode is useful for any team that needs internal or public documentation of an AI system's properties, regardless of regulatory obligation.

   rationale...
