## What it does

The Incident Response Logger takes a description of an AI system failure, unexpected output, or harm event and structures it into a complete incident report aligned to the NIST SP 800-61 incident response lifecycle and EU AI Act Article 62 serious incident reporting requirements. It determines whether the incident meets the Article 62 serious incident threshold, identifies the required notification timeline and recipients (internal, national market surveillance authority, the Commission), and produces a pre-filled report ready for legal sign-off and, where required, regulatory submission.

## Frameworks covered

- NIST SP 800-61 Rev. 2 — Computer Security Incident Handling Guide (four-phase lifecycle)
- EU AI Act Article 62 — reporting of serious incidents by providers of high-risk AI systems
- EU AI Act Recital 97 — serious incident definition and proportionality
- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Clause 10 — continual improvement and nonconformity handling

## When to use it

Reach for this skill the moment an AI system produces an output that causes or could cause harm to a person, a group, or critical infrastructure — or when a system behaves in a way that is materially outside its documented intended purpose. In the EU, providers of high-risk AI systems must report serious incidents to the national market surveillance authority without undue delay and within 15 days of becoming aware (EU AI Act Article 62). This skill ensures the right information is captured in the right structure before the clock runs out. It also supports post-incident reviews, where the structured log becomes the basis for root-cause analysis and AIMS corrective action.
