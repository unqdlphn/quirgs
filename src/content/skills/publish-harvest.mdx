## What it does

Publish Harvest runs a structured quarterly performance review across the full catalog. It pulls key metrics from the reconciled royalty ledger (publish-income output), categorizes every track into one of four performance tiers — Growth, Stable, Declining, or Dormant — and surfaces concentration risk, revenue anomalies, and sync licensing gaps.

The review calculates which tracks are generating disproportionate revenue (top-4 concentration), which are trending down quarter-over-quarter, and where the catalog has holes relative to active brief types. It closes with a tiered harvest report: specific recommended actions for each performance segment, from investment decisions on Growth tracks to retirement or catalog re-pitch decisions on Dormant ones.

## Frameworks covered

- Catalog performance tiering (Growth, Stable, Declining, Dormant)
- Revenue concentration analysis
- Streaming velocity and trend detection
- Sync revenue benchmarking against catalog size
- Quarter-over-quarter comparison methodology

## When to use it

Run Publish Harvest at the end of every quarter, after publish-income has reconciled the period's statements into a clean ledger. It's the strategic layer that sits above the transactional reconciliation — it answers not just "what came in" but "what does this mean for the catalog." Use it before making catalog development decisions, investment prioritization calls, or sync pitch list updates. Also use it when the owner wants a single document summarizing catalog health for an investor, partner, or board conversation.
