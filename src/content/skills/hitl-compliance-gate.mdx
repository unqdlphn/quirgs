## What it does

The HITL Compliance Gate generates a structured sign-off checklist that a human reviewer works through before AI-generated output is allowed to proceed — to publication, deployment, user delivery, or any downstream system. It maps each checklist item to a specific control from EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, or ISO/IEC 42001:2023, so reviewers know exactly what they are confirming and why it matters. Each item carries a pass / fail / pending status and a notes field for the reviewer's observations. When `QUIRGS_HITL_WRITE_TOKEN` is set, the skill automatically posts the checkpoint to the Quirgs HITL Gate Worker and holds output pending human sign-off before proceeding.

## Frameworks covered

- EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) — Articles 9, 13, 14, 16
- NIST AI RMF 1.0 — Govern, Measure, and Manage functions
- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — Clauses 6, 8, and 9 (planning, operations, performance evaluation)

## When to use it

Insert a compliance gate at any moment where human judgment must verify AI output before it advances. The most common trigger points are pre-deployment (before a model goes to production), pre-publication (before AI-generated content reaches an audience), and pre-release (before a new AI feature ships to users). It's also useful mid-workflow when a reviewer needs a structured record of what they checked and approved — not just a verbal sign-off.

## Example output (abbreviated)

```
HITL COMPLIANCE GATE — SIGN-OFF CHECKLIST
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Workflow: AI-generated customer support responses
Gate type: Pre-publication
Reviewer: [Name / Role]
Date: 2026-05-21

EU AI ACT
  ✅ PASS   — Output does not constitute a prohibited practice (Article 5)
  ✅ PASS   — Human oversight mechanism is active and able to intervene (Article 14)
  ⚠️  PENDING — Transparency notice provided to end users confirming AI involvement (Article 13)
                Notes: Notice draft under legal review — gate holds until confirmed

NIST AI RMF
  ✅ PASS   — Output reviewed against documented acceptable use policy (GV-1.1)
  ❌ FAIL   — Adverse output detection logging not yet active (MG-2.4)
                Notes: Logging ticket open — do not advance to production

ISO/IEC 42001
  ✅ PASS   — AI system objective and scope documented (Clause 4.3)
  ✅ PASS   — Monitoring and evaluation plan in place (Clause 9.1)

GATE STATUS: HOLD — resolve FAIL items before proceeding
```
