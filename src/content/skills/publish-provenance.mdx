## What it does

Publish Provenance generates a formal provenance record for a music release, documenting the creative process and assessing the degree of AI involvement using the Provenance Triangle framework. The Triangle evaluates three dimensions: human authorship (original creative decisions made by a human), AI tool use (which tools were used and at what involvement tier), and synthesis degree (how much of the final output derives from AI generation versus human performance and arrangement).

Each tool used in the production chain is mapped to an AI involvement tier: Tier 0 (traditional tools, no AI), Tier 1 (AI-assisted, human-primary), Tier 2 (AI-generative, substantially rewritten or arranged by human), Tier 3 (AI-primary, human-curated), or Tier 4 (AI-generated voice/likeness without authorization — hard stop). The assessment produces a copyright-readiness determination and a disclosure label appropriate for the platform, PRO, or sync submission requiring it.

## Frameworks covered

- Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. §102) — human authorship requirement for copyright registration
- EU AI Act Article 13 — transparency obligations for AI-generated content
- Platform AI disclosure policies (Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, TikTok, DistroKid)
- Provenance Triangle Framework — Tier 0–4 AI involvement classification
- USCO AI registration guidance (2024)

## When to use it

Run Publish Provenance for every release that used any AI tool in the production chain — even Tier 1 tools like AI-assisted mastering. The record is required before publish-broadcast (PRO registration increasingly requires AI disclosure) and before publish-license (sync supervisors ask about AI use on submission forms). It also creates the audit trail needed for platform takedown disputes and copyright challenges.

For Tier 3 tracks, the record surfaces whether the release can claim copyright protection at all — and documents the creative decisions that support any claim. For Tier 4, the skill issues a hard stop and routes to the appropriate legal escalation.
