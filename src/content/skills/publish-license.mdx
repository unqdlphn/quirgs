## What it does

Publish License runs a three-agent pipeline — Brief Analyst, Catalog Matcher, Pitch Writer — to take a sync licensing opportunity from brief intake to submission-ready pitch in a single pass.

The Brief Analyst decodes the creative, technical, and rights requirements in the brief: mood, tempo, instrumentation, duration, exclusivity, territory, and any restrictions (no lyrics, no AI-generated content, specific genre exclusions). The Catalog Matcher scores every eligible track in the catalog against those parameters and returns a ranked shortlist with per-criterion scores and a confidence rating. The Pitch Writer converts the shortlist into a polished pitch document formatted for the specific placement type — film, TV, advertising, or game — ready to send directly to the music supervisor.

When the catalog has no strong match for a brief, the skill documents the gap explicitly rather than forcing a low-confidence recommendation.

## Frameworks covered

- Sync licensing protocol — master use and sync rights clearance
- Music supervisor submission standards
- Blanket license and per-placement licensing structures
- AI content restrictions (platform-specific, per brief requirements)

## When to use it

Use Publish License whenever a sync brief arrives. It handles everything from small indie placements to major advertising campaigns. Run publish-shield first if any candidate tracks have unresolved rights questions — the Catalog Matcher enforces a hard stop on tracks with active clearance blocks, regardless of how well they score against the brief. Also use it in reverse: when a supervisor asks for a specific type of track you don't have, the gap analysis output feeds directly into catalog development planning.
