## What it does

The PII Exposure Checker audits your AI system's data inputs, training datasets, and inference pipeline for personally identifiable information exposure risks. For each finding, it assigns a risk rating (critical / high / medium / low), maps the exposure to the specific GDPR provision or EU AI Act Article 10 control that applies, and recommends a concrete remediation action. The output is a structured exposure report — not just a flag list — that is ready for a data protection officer review or an Article 10 compliance audit.

## Frameworks covered

- GDPR — Articles 5, 6, 9, 25, and 35 (lawful basis, special category data, data minimization, privacy by design, DPIA)
- EU AI Act Article 10 — data and data governance requirements for high-risk AI
- EU AI Act Recital 44 — bias in training data
- ISO/IEC 27701:2019 — privacy information management (supplementary)

## When to use it

Run this audit before training a new model on any dataset that originates from real-world user activity, customer records, health systems, or scraped web content. It's also the right tool before deploying an existing model in a new context where inference inputs may contain personal data not present in the original training scope. EU AI Act Article 10 requires that high-risk AI systems use training, validation, and test data that are relevant, representative, and free from errors — and the data governance documentation must demonstrate it. This skill produces the evidence that supports that claim.

## Example output (abbreviated)

```
PII EXPOSURE REPORT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
System: Medical triage support model
Dataset audited: intake_forms_2023_cleaned.csv
Audit date: 2026-05-21

FINDING #1
  Risk rating: CRITICAL
  PII type: Special category — health data (GDPR Article 9)
  Location: Column "chief_complaint" — free-text field containing patient-reported
            symptoms, medications, and prior diagnoses
  Applicable control: GDPR Art. 9(2) — no explicit consent or qualifying basis
                      documented for processing this category
                      EU AI Act Art. 10(3) — special category data requires
                      strict necessity justification and enhanced safeguards
  Remediation: Pseudonymise free-text field before training; document legal basis
               under Art. 9(2)(h) (medical purposes) with DPO sign-off;
               run DPIA (Art. 35) before model deployment

FINDING #2
  Risk rating: HIGH
  PII type: Direct identifier — full name + date of birth combination
  Location: Columns "patient_name", "dob"
  Applicable control: GDPR Art. 5(1)(c) — data minimisation
  Remediation: Drop both columns; replace with anonymised patient ID token
```
